Understanding NDVI

Learn how satellites measure vegetation health from space

What is NDVI?

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) is a scientific index used to measure vegetation health using satellite imagery.

It shows how green and healthy plants are.

  • High NDVI → Healthy vegetation
  • Low NDVI → Dry or unhealthy vegetation
  • Negative NDVI → Water, buildings, snow

NDVI Formula

NDVI = (NIR − RED) / (NIR + RED)

Where:

  • NIR → Near Infrared light
  • RED → Red light

Healthy plants reflect more NIR and absorb Red light.

NDVI Value Meaning

NDVI Value Meaning
0.6 to 1.0 Very Healthy Vegetation
0.4 to 0.6 Healthy Vegetation
0.2 to 0.4 Moderate Vegetation
0 to 0.2 Poor Vegetation
Less than 0 Water / Urban

Why NDVI is Important?

  • Agriculture monitoring
  • Drought detection
  • Forest health analysis
  • Climate change studies
  • Satellite vegetation monitoring
